On-device extraction is the default whenever this machine supports it: the photo never leaves your computer. Sending it to a cloud provider instead requires a key above and ticking the consent box for each import. Crop out any personally identifiable information before uploading — a cloud image is subject to that provider's terms of service and privacy policy.

reading chart…

No key needed and the photo never leaves your device — Chrome's on-device model reads it locally. Works best on reports with a printed threshold table (like ABR/BERA results); for plotted charts, use a provider key above. First use downloads the model (one time, a few GB).

Applying is not final — open the extension popup to review each point on the chart and drag anything the model misread.

Calibration

Without calibration the per-band gains are internally consistent but not absolute: what reaches the ear depends on your headphones and volume. Two helpers, both optional. Neither is clinical probe-microphone verification.

generic average shapes, not measurements

Tone match: use your usual headphones at your usual volume. Play the 1 kHz tone as the anchor, then each other tone; drag its slider until it sounds equally loud. The offsets become part of the correction the player applies.

Measurement-mic correction: the repo's calibrate_playback.py measures your actual playback response with a USB measurement mic and writes a correction JSON — import it here.
